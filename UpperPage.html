Upper Body Workouts

  • Bicep Curl: Starting with arms at the side, bring the lower arms up to form a 90 degree angle flex in the arm, then return to the starting position.
  • Pull Up (will require a stable, horizontal bar suspended above): Hang from the bar via hands, then bring your arms to a 90 degree angle, and return to the hanging position.
  • Doorwar Curl: Holding on the the side of a doorway and leaning back with arms extended, pull your body to the door to flex your arms to 90 degree bends, then return to arms extended.
  • Push Up: Hold yourself above the ground using your toes and extended arms (arms below your shoulders), bend the arms to lower the top half of your body to the ground, then push to return your arms to full extension.
  • Bench Press: Laying on your back, suspend a weight above your body with arms fully extended, lower the weight until your arms are flex at 90 degrees, then return your arms to full extension, lifting the weight.
  • Tricep Extensions: hold a weight above your head with arms extended, flex your arms until they reach 90 degrees and the weight is above and behind your head, then straighten your arms to bring the weight back above your head.
  • Lateral Arm Raises: Holding weights in hand with arms at your side, raise your hands to form a 'T' shape with your body, then return your arms to your side.
  • Frontal Arm Raises: Holding weights in hand with arms at your side, raise your hands out infront of you until you make an upside down 'L' with your body, the nreturn your arms to your side.
  • Posterior Arm Raises: Holding weights in hand with arms at your side, lean forward slightly and raise your hand behind your body, then return your arms to your sides.
  • Pull Up: (will require a stable, horizontal bar suspended above): Hang from the bar via hands, then bring your arms to a 90 degree angle, and return to the hanging position.
  • Butterfly Row: laying on your stomach on a bench, hold weights in your hands below you, then raise them to your sides, making a 'T' out of your body, then return them below you.
  • Bodyweight Row: Lay a broom or strong stick between two chairs so there is a gap between the chairs, lay beneath the stick and with both hands and a straight back, pull yourself up to the chair, then return down to arms fully extended.
  • Push up: Hold yourself above the ground using your toes and extended arms, bend the arms to lower the top half of your body to the ground, then push to return your arms to full extension.
  • Butterfly press: Lay on your back in the 'T' position with weights in your hands, then bring your hands above your body while keeping your arms straight, then return to the 'T' position and weight on the floor.
  • Wide Arm Push Up: Hold yourself above the ground using your toes and extended arms (arms out widers than your shoulders), bend the arms to lower the top half of your body to the ground, then push to return your arms to full extension.
  • Shrugs: Hold your arms at your sides with weights in your hands, the raise your shoulders up without bending your arms, then return them back down.
  • Push Up: Hold yourself above the ground using your foes and extended arms, bend the arms to lower the top half of your body to the ground, then push to return your arms to full extension.
  • Upright Row: hold your arms at your sides with weights in your hands, then pull your hands as high as you can without moving them away from your body, then return them back to your sides.
